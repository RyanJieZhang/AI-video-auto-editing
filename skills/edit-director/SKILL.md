# Edit Director

Use this skill to turn footage and script into an editorial plan with taste. It owns the cut logic, not asset acquisition or rendering.

## Responsibilities

- Choose the strongest opening shot or opening visual.
- Decide shot order, pacing, cut length, and rhythm.
- Mark fast cuts, holds, cutaways, inserts, reaction moments, and proof moments.
- Decide what to remove.
- Plan J-cuts, L-cuts, ambience bridges, and visual resets when audio exists.
- Create a high-quality edit plan that implementation can follow.

## Workflow

1. Read `plan/script.md`, `plan/shot-list.md`, `plan/asset-list.md`, and `plan/creative-direction.md`.
2. Identify the video's retention beats.
3. Choose the first 3 seconds and the strongest visual sequence.
4. Build an edit decision list.
5. Mark moments that need motion graphics, captions, or sound hits.
6. Write `plan/edit-decision-list.md`.
7. Update `plan/timeline.md` only when the edit logic changes.

## Output

```md
# Edit Decision List

| Time | Shot/Asset | Edit Decision | Reason | Audio | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
```

## Quality Heuristics

- The first 3 seconds must earn attention.
- Do not use a shot just because it exists.
- Cut on meaning, action, rhythm, or audio energy.
- Let premium shots breathe; cut weak shots fast or remove them.
- Avoid constant motion if the viewer needs to read.

## Do Not

- Do not download assets.
- Do not perform final color grading.
- Do not implement code.
- Do not accept every footage segment as usable.
